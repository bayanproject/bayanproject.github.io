What Bayan project does?

Aim

  • To develop a reliable algorithm for a robust detection of communities in networks, with optimality guarantees.

Community Detection

  • Community detection is the process of inductively identifying groups within a network (graph). It is a core problem in data science with a wide range of applications in life sciences (e.g. biological systems), social sciences (social networks), physicial sciences (complex systems), mathematical sciences (informational networks), and even health sciences (drug discovery) (Wikipedia).
  • There are several existing methods and algorithms for approaching this computational problem. One family of algorithms attempts to find communities by maximizing modularity: A network-level quantity indicating the fitness of inferred communities in being internally cohesive and mutually separated.

Do we need yet another community detection algorithm? Yes, and here's why:

  • In the first step of this project, we used the following exact integer programming model alongside existing heuristic methods for maximizing modularity in a testbed of real social networks and random networks.
  • We evaluated the gap in modularity between the integer programming globally optimal solution and several existing modularity-based community detection methods, CNM, Louvain, Combo, Belief, non-negative matrix factorization, and Leiden.

    The optimality gap for the two algorithms Belief and non-negative matrix factorization were consistently larger than 20% and hence they have been ommitted from this plot. According to this evaluation, the best performing heuristic algorithm, in terms of proximity to an optimal solution, is Combo (Sobolevsky et al. 2014) which is the only heuristic with an optimality gap lower than 5% for both real and synthetic networks in our experiments.

  • We also measured the level of similarity between the sub-optimal communities from each heuristic method and the optimal modularity-maximizing communities using the concept of normalized mutual information (NMI). NMI represents the degree of the match between a pair of network partitions on a scale of 0 to 1 (e.g. NMI takes the value 1 for two identical partitions).
  • This simple analysis indicates a susbtantial gap (1-NMI) between the partitions obtained using the current heuristic methods and a true modularity-maximizing partition of those networks.
  • Bayan algorithm solves this fundamental problem and provides partitions within a guaranteed proximity of the maximum modularity. The Bayan algorithm will soon be publicly available as a part of the Community Discovery Library (CDlib) for Python alongside a wrapper for R users.

Origin of the algorithm name "Bayan"?

  • Bayan is a village in the Southeast of Iran located at the midpoint of the hometowns of the developers of this project.
  • Bayan is a small "community" of 22 people in 5 families as of the 2016 census.
